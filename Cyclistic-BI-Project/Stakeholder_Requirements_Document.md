# Stakeholder Requirements Document: Cyclistic BI Project

**BI Professional:** Timilehin Afolayan  
**Client/Sponsor:** Jamal Harris, Director, Customer Data

## Business Problem
Cyclistic's Customer Growth Team is currently developing a business plan for the upcoming year and needs a comprehensive understanding of how customers use their bikes. The primary priority for this initiative is identifying customer demand at different station locations. Ultimately, the team aims to apply these customer usage insights to inform strategic decisions regarding new station growth.

## Stakeholders
The major stakeholders for this project include Sara Romero, who serves as the VP of Marketing. Another key stakeholder is Ernest Cox, the VP of Product Development. Jamal Harris, the Director of Customer Data, is also a primary sponsor and stakeholder. Finally, Nina Locklear, the Director of Procurement, is involved in the project.

## Stakeholder Usage Details
The Customer Growth Team will use this BI tool to gain actionable insights from the data generated by customers using Cyclistic bikes. The resulting dashboard will help stakeholders understand customer preferences, identify the characteristics of a successful product, and determine how new stations could alleviate demand in various geographical areas. This information will be critical in guiding decisions on how to effectively develop and place new station locations.

## Primary Requirements
In order for this project to be successful, the BI tool must meet several key requirements. First, it must include a table or map visualization exploring starting and ending station locations, aggregated by location. Second, it needs a visualization showing which destination locations are most popular based on total trip minutes. Third, the tool must provide a visualization focusing on trends from the summer of 2015. Fourth, it requires a visualization showing the percent growth in trips year over year. Additionally, the tool must gather and present insights about congestion at stations, the total number of trips across all starting and ending locations, and peak usage patterns segmented by time of day, season, and weather impact.
